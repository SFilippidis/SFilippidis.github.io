Dr. Stavros K. Filippidis is an Informatics & Mathematics teacher, a researcher, and a software developer. His research focuses on New Educational Technologies. He is very interested in free software development, Byzantine Church Music, and filmmaking.

He earned a Ph.D. from the department of Informatics (2008), an M.Sc. in Medical Informatics (2002), a B.Sc. in Informatics (2000), and a B.Sc. in Mathematics (1996), all of them from Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, Greece.

He has worked as an adjunct lecturer with the department of Informatics (2010, Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, Greece), as an adjunct lecturer with the department of Philosophy and Pedagogy (2009, Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, Greece), as a laboratory collaborator with the department of Informatics (2003-2008, Technological Educational Institute of Thessaloniki, Greece), as a laboratory collaborator with the department of Logistics (2005-2006, Technological Educational Institute of Thessaloniki, Greece), and as a seconded high school teacher with the department of Logistics (2006-2009, Technological Educational Institute of Thessaloniki, Greece).

He is a high school teacher (2001-present), and currently he is teaching Informatics and Mathematics at the Music School of Katerini.

He is married and he is the father of three children.
